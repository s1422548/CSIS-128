Modern West Coast Hip-Hop

Modern west coast hip-hop takes a lot of influences from old school west coast hip-hop.The G-Funk style of hip-hop can still be heard today. The G-Funk is now usually mixed in with a lot more modern sounds such as trap beats and old soul music samples. These new west coast rappers are about as popular as the old school rappers were in their time. Below are some artists that fit into the modern west coast hip-hop genre.

Kendrick Lamar


Kendrick Lamar is a rapper from Compton, California. He is one of the most successful rappers that is still producing music. Kendrick has only been in the public eye for about 4 or 5 years and has already received seven Grammys in that time. In 2015, he was nominated for the second most Grammys by any artist in one year, second to only Michael Jackson. His music is mainly about his experiences growing up in Compton and the struggles he went through everyday.

Schoolboy Q


Quincy Hanley AKA Schoolboy Q is a rapper from Los Angeles, California. He is a member of Top Dawg Entertainment along with Kendrick Lamar and several other artists. Besides Kendrick, Schoolboy Q is the only member of TDE to have an album debut at number one on the Billboard 200 charts. He is known for his very aggresive flow and very dark song content. His appeal comes from his grittiness and his variety of musical styles he uses.

YG


Keenon Jackson AKA YG is a rapper from Compton, California. Out of all the current west coast rappers, YG has arguably had the most radio success. Many of his songs have become chart toppers and have received a lot of radio play. He's done this by using the classic G-Funk sound and making it into it's own modern style. If you didn't know what era he was from, it would probably be very hard to guess it correctly. YG is known for his laidback, low-pitched flow.
